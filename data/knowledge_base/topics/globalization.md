# Globalization - IELTS Writing Task 2
# 全球化

## 常见题型 (Common Question Types)

1. **Globalization is creating a more homogeneous world. Is this a positive or negative development?**
   全球化正在创造一个更加同质化的世界。这是积极的还是消极的发展？

2. **Some people believe that international trade and cultural exchange benefit everyone, while others think they disadvantage local communities. Discuss both views.**
   一些人认为国际贸易和文化交流使每个人受益，而另一些人则认为它们损害了当地社区。讨论双方观点。

3. **Multinational companies are becoming increasingly powerful. Is this a positive or negative development for society?**
   跨国公司变得越来越强大。这对社会是积极的还是消极的发展？

4. **Countries are losing their cultural identity because of globalization. Do you agree or disagree?**
   国家正在因全球化而失去文化认同。你同意还是不同意？

5. **Some people argue that we should buy local products rather than imported goods to protect local economies. To what extent do you agree?**
   一些人认为我们应该购买本地产品而非进口商品以保护地方经济。你在多大程度上同意？

6. **Global problems such as climate change and pandemics require global solutions. Discuss.**
   气候变化和疫情等全球问题需要全球解决方案。请讨论。

## 核心论点 (Core Arguments)

### 支持方论点 (Arguments For)

- **Argument: Globalization lifts millions out of poverty through trade.**
  - Logic: Developing countries access global markets → Foreign investment creates jobs → Technology and knowledge transfer → Rising incomes → China and India examples → Hundreds of millions entered middle class in three decades
  - 中文：发展中国家进入全球市场 → 外国投资创造就业 → 技术和知识转移 → 收入增加 → 中国和印度的例子 → 30年间数亿人进入中产阶级

- **Argument: Cultural exchange enriches rather than erases.**
  - Logic: Exposure to diverse traditions broadens perspectives → Fusion creates new art, music, cuisine → Cultural exchange is not zero-sum → Local cultures adapt and evolve anyway → Global awareness fosters tolerance and reduces xenophobia
  - 中文：接触多元传统开阔视野 → 融合创造新的艺术、音乐、美食 → 文化交流不是零和的 → 本土文化本来就在适应和演变 → 全球意识培养宽容并减少仇外心理

- **Argument: Global cooperation is essential for transnational problems.**
  - Logic: Climate change, pandemics, terrorism ignore borders → No single nation can solve them alone → International bodies (WHO, UN, IPCC) coordinate responses → Shared research accelerates solutions → Vaccine development during COVID-19 proved unprecedented global scientific collaboration
  - 中文：气候变化、疫情、恐怖主义无视国界 → 任何国家都无法独自解决它们 → 国际机构（世卫组织、联合国、IPCC）协调应对 → 共享研究加速解决方案 → 新冠疫情期间的疫苗研发证明了前所未有的全球科学合作

- **Argument: Multinational companies drive innovation and efficiency.**
  - Logic: Global competition forces continuous improvement → Economies of scale reduce costs → Cross-border R&D tackles complex problems → Standards of living rise through cheaper, better products → Access to medicines and technology spreads worldwide
  - 中文：全球竞争迫使不断改进 → 规模经济降低成本 → 跨境研发解决复杂问题 → 通过更便宜、更好的产品提高生活水平 → 药品和技术的获取在全球范围内传播

### 反对方论点 (Arguments Against)

- **Argument: Globalization exacerbates inequality within nations.**
  - Logic: Capital is mobile; labor is not → Companies move production to lowest-cost countries → Manufacturing jobs lost in developed nations → Rust Belt phenomenon → Workers in wealthy countries left behind → Political backlash and populism
  - 中文：资本是流动的；劳动力不是 → 公司将生产转移到成本最低的国家 → 发达国家制造业岗位流失 → 铁锈地带现象 → 富裕国家的工人被抛在后面 → 政治反弹和民粹主义

- **Argument: Cultural homogenization destroys unique traditions.**
  - Logic: Western (especially American) brands dominate globally → Local businesses and crafts cannot compete → Indigenous languages dying at accelerating rates → Youth culture converges on globalized media → Loss of centuries-old customs and knowledge systems → World becomes culturally poorer
  - 中文：西方（尤其是美国）品牌主导全球 → 本地企业和手工艺无法竞争 → 土著语言以加速的速度消亡 → 青年文化趋同于全球化的媒体 → 数百年习俗和知识体系的丧失 → 世界在文化上变得更贫乏

- **Argument: Multinational corporations exploit weak regulations.**
  - Logic: Tax avoidance through profit shifting → Environmental dumping in countries with lax laws → Labor exploitation in supply chains (sweatshops) → Local businesses crushed by predatory pricing → Profits concentrated among shareholders in wealthy nations → Race to the bottom in standards
  - 中文：通过利润转移避税 → 在法律宽松的国家进行环境倾销 → 供应链中的劳动剥削（血汗工厂）→ 掠夺性定价击垮本地企业 → 利润集中在富裕国家的股东手中 → 标准的逐底竞争

- **Argument: Global supply chains create systemic vulnerability.**
  - Logic: Interdependence means shock in one region cascades globally → COVID-19 exposed fragility of concentrated manufacturing → Chip shortage crippled multiple industries → Geopolitical tension threatens resource access → Food security compromised when imports disrupted → Efficiency prioritized over resilience
  - 中文：相互依赖意味着一地的冲击会全球级联 → 新冠疫情暴露了集中制造的脆弱性 → 芯片短缺瘫痪了多个行业 → 地缘政治紧张威胁资源获取 → 进口中断时粮食安全受到损害 → 效率优先于韧性

## 典型例子 (Typical Examples)

1. **China's economic transformation** — Since joining the WTO in 2001, China lifted over 800 million people out of poverty through export-led growth. Simultaneously, manufacturing job losses in developed countries created the political conditions for trade wars and protectionist backlash. The double-edged nature of globalization.
   中国的经济转型——自2001年加入WTO以来，中国通过出口导向型增长使超过8亿人摆脱贫困。同时，发达国家的制造业岗位流失为贸易战和保护主义反弹创造了政治条件。全球化的双刃性。

2. **The Rana Plaza collapse (2013, Bangladesh)** — A garment factory supplying Western fashion brands collapsed, killing 1,134 workers; exposed the human cost of fast fashion supply chains. Sparked global movements for ethical sourcing and transparency. Illustrates multinational power without accountability.
   拉纳广场倒塌事件（2013年，孟加拉国）——一家为西方时尚品牌供货的服装厂倒塌，造成1,134名工人死亡；暴露了快时尚供应链的人力成本。引发了全球道德采购和透明度的运动。说明没有问责的跨国权力。

3. **UNESCO's intangible cultural heritage protection** — Listing of traditions like Japanese washoku cuisine, Argentine tango, and Indian yoga protects cultural practices from being overwhelmed by globalization. Shows that global institutions can preserve diversity while embracing interconnection.
   联合国教科文组织的非物质文化遗产保护——将日本和食、阿根廷探戈和印度瑜伽等传统列入名录，保护文化实践免于被全球化淹没。展示全球机构可以在拥抱互联的同时保护多样性。

4. **The global microchip shortage (2020-2023)** — Over-reliance on Taiwan (TSMC) for advanced semiconductors created a single point of failure when pandemic disruptions hit; automotive, consumer electronics, and defense industries worldwide were paralyzed. A stark lesson in supply chain concentration risk.
   全球微芯片短缺（2020-2023年）——对台湾（台积电）先进半导体的过度依赖在疫情中断时创造了单一故障点；全球汽车、消费电子和国防工业瘫痪。供应链集中风险的惨痛教训。
