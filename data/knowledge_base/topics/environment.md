# Environment - IELTS Writing Task 2
# 环境

## 常见题型 (Common Question Types)

1. **Climate change is the greatest threat facing humanity. To what extent do you agree?**
   气候变化是人类面临的最大威胁。你在多大程度上同意？

2. **Environmental problems are too big for individuals to solve; only governments and large companies can make a difference. Do you agree or disagree?**
   环境问题太大，个人无法解决；只有政府和大公司才能有所作为。你同意还是不同意？

3. **The use of plastic bags and packaging should be banned worldwide. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages.**
   塑料袋和包装应在全球范围内禁止使用。讨论其优点和缺点。

4. **Economic growth is more important than protecting the environment. Do you agree or disagree?**
   经济增长比保护环境更重要。你同意还是不同意？

5. **International cooperation is essential to protect the environment. To what extent do you agree?**
   国际合作对于保护环境至关重要。你在多大程度上同意？

6. **Some people argue that the best way to reduce pollution is to increase the cost of fuel. Do you think this is an effective solution?**
   一些人认为减少污染的最佳方法是提高燃料成本。你认为这是一个有效的解决方案吗？

## 核心论点 (Core Arguments)

### 支持方论点 (Arguments For)

- **Argument: Environmental protection secures long-term economic stability.**
  - Logic: Natural disasters from climate change cost billions annually → Crop failures, infrastructure damage, health crises → Prevention is cheaper than recovery → A healthy environment underpins all economic activity
  - 中文：气候变化引发的自然灾害每年造成数十亿美元损失 → 作物歉收、基础设施损坏、健康危机 → 预防比恢复更便宜 → 健康的环境支撑着所有经济活动

- **Argument: Individual actions collectively create significant impact.**
  - Logic: Billions of small daily choices → Reduced plastic, energy, water consumption → Market signals shift corporate behavior → Governments emboldened to legislate → Systemic change from the bottom up
  - 中文：数十亿个日常小选择 → 减少塑料、能源、水消耗 → 市场信号改变企业行为 → 政府更有底气立法 → 自下而上的系统性变革

- **Argument: Strict regulation drives green innovation.**
  - Logic: Legal requirements create market demand for clean tech → Companies invest in R&D to comply → Breakthrough innovations emerge → Costs fall as technology matures → Green solutions become economically competitive
  - 中文：法律要求创造清洁技术的市场需求 → 企业投资研发以合规 → 突破性创新涌现 → 技术成熟后成本下降 → 绿色解决方案在经济上变得具有竞争力

- **Argument: Environmental degradation disproportionately harms the poor.**
  - Logic: Low-income communities live near pollution sources → Lack resources to adapt → Health impacts trap families in poverty → Environmental protection is social justice → Moral imperative for action
  - 中文：低收入社区靠近污染源 → 缺乏适应资源 → 健康影响使家庭陷入贫困 → 环境保护是社会正义 → 行动的道德义务

### 反对方论点 (Arguments Against)

- **Argument: Aggressive environmental policies hurt economic competitiveness.**
  - Logic: Strict regulations increase production costs → Companies relocate to lax jurisdictions → Job losses in regulated countries → Carbon leakage — emissions simply shift, not reduce → Global emissions unchanged
  - 中文：严格法规增加生产成本 → 企业迁往监管宽松的地区 → 受监管国家的就业损失 → 碳泄漏——排放仅仅是转移而非减少 → 全球排放不变

- **Argument: Banning plastics creates unintended consequences.**
  - Logic: Alternative materials (glass, metal, paper) have their own environmental costs → Heavier packaging increases transport emissions → Food waste rises without plastic preservation → Blanket bans ignore nuanced lifecycle analysis
  - 中文：替代材料（玻璃、金属、纸）有其自身的环境成本 → 更重的包装增加运输排放 → 没有塑料保鲜导致食物浪费增加 → 一刀切的禁令忽视了精细的生命周期分析

- **Argument: Focusing on environment slows poverty alleviation in developing nations.**
  - Logic: Industrialization lifted billions from poverty historically → Developing nations need cheap energy → Coal remains most affordable → Demanding green transitions now is hypocritical from nations that already industrialized through fossil fuels
  - 中文：历史上工业化使数十亿人摆脱贫困 → 发展中国家需要廉价能源 → 煤炭仍然最便宜 → 那些已经通过化石燃料实现工业化的国家现在要求绿色转型是虚伪的

## 典型例子 (Typical Examples)

1. **The Paris Agreement (2015)** — 196 countries committed to limiting global temperature rise to well below 2 degrees Celsius. Highlights both the power and limitations of international cooperation: ambitious goals, but national implementation varies widely and enforcement mechanisms remain weak.
   《巴黎协定》（2015年）——196个国家承诺将全球气温上升限制在远低于2摄氏度。突显国际合作的力量和局限：目标雄心勃勃，但各国执行差异大，执行机制薄弱。

2. **Rwanda's plastic bag ban** — In 2008, Rwanda became one of the first countries to ban single-use plastic bags entirely; Kigali is now one of Africa's cleanest capitals. Demonstrates that strict policy combined with enforcement and public education can work even in developing nations.
   卢旺达的塑料袋禁令——2008年，卢旺达成为最早完全禁止一次性塑料袋的国家之一；基加利现在是非洲最干净的首都之一。证明即使是发展中国家，严格政策配合执法和公众教育也能奏效。

3. **Costa Rica's renewable energy success** — Over 98% of electricity generated from renewable sources (hydro, geothermal, wind, solar); tourism boomed partly due to eco-friendly reputation. Shows that environmental leadership and economic prosperity can coexist.
   哥斯达黎加的可再生能源成功——超过98%的电力来自可再生能源（水能、地热、风能、太阳能）；旅游业因环保声誉而蓬勃发展。展示环境领导力与经济繁荣可以共存。

4. **The Great Pacific Garbage Patch** — A floating mass of plastic debris spanning 1.6 million square kilometers in the Pacific Ocean; marine life ingests microplastics entering the human food chain. Vividly illustrates the global scale and interconnectedness of pollution.
   大太平洋垃圾带——太平洋中面积达160万平方公里的漂浮塑料碎片群；海洋生物摄入微塑料进入人类食物链。生动说明污染的全球规模和相互关联性。
